Priscilla Kim

Life as a freelance writer from a resort in the French Alps.

Illustratror

Vietnam

Undisclosed

What are you working on and where are you located?

I freelance for various companies and individuals, mostly gaming-related, occasionally fantasy/sf magazines or self-published authors and private commissions. Currently I'm in Vietnam, but I'm only here for a week or two on a sightseeing basis before I move on to Taiwan for a month.

How did you end up working remotely?

I first heard the term "digital nomad" a few years ago, and decided I wanted to do that. So I saved up for a couple of years, doing freelance and establishing contacts in my free time outside of my day job. When I felt I had enough to provide a good runway, I quit my day job and started traveling.

What are the largest challenges you've encountered?

There's always the challenge of figuring out how the basics of daily life work in the place you're currently in, but mostly my biggest challenge is balancing sightseeing versus work time. I move very fast (mostly because I want to squeeze in a lot of places while I can), so it's a bit harder for me compared to someone who might travel slow and only move every few months. The second-biggest challenge is sometimes figuring out where I can find decent wifi and outlets.

"The second-biggest challenge is sometimes figuring out where I can find decent wifi and outlets."

Where is the most interesting place you’ve ever worked from?

Interesting or "interesting?" ;) Probably the most aggravating was working in a hostel lobby in Rotorua, because I kept getting beaned by ping pong balls whenever the players at the nearby table missed. On the other hand, that was where I finished up Keeper of the Past in time to submit for Spectrum Fantastic Art 24 (and made it in!), so I have some fond memories of it in hindsight.

What time zone do you keep?

Whatever my current one is. With my freelance work, I don't need to keep a synchronous time schedule.

What gear do you use on a day to day basis? Is there anything that makes your job easier?

Aside from daily life stuff (clothes, toiletries, phone, wallet), mostly just my Cintiq Companion and the assorted peripherals that go with it. I literally couldn't do my job without it, and it's easier traveling with a tablet PC I can paint directly on than with a laptop and separate drawing tablet.

Do you have any advice for anyone thinking about going remote or just starting out? Go for it! There's a lot of remote work out there.

Have a decent amount saved up (at least 6 months' worth wherever you'll be living) and a handful of established clients in your field. More risk-taking people might be comfortable with less, but that's worked pretty well for me, as a fairly risk-averse person.

What’s next for you? (Location, remote vs office, etc.)

More travel! I'll be sitting pretty in Taiwan for about a month meeting a lot of deadlines before I embark on another series of short trips around East and SE Asia.
